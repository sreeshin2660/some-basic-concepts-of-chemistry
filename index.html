Key Concepts

Atomic Structure

Atoms consist of protons, neutrons, and electrons. The atomic number represents the number of protons, while the mass number is the sum of protons and neutrons.

The Bohr model explains electron orbits, while the quantum mechanical model describes electron probability distributions.

Stoichiometry

Stoichiometry deals with the quantitative relationships between reactants and products in a chemical reaction. The mole concept helps in calculating amounts of substances.

Key formulas:

  • Mole = Given mass / Molar mass
  • Moles of gas = Volume at STP / 22.4 L

Chemical Bonding

Chemical bonds form due to electrostatic forces between atoms. Types include ionic, covalent, and metallic bonds.

VSEPR theory helps predict molecular shapes, while hybridization explains atomic orbital mixing.
